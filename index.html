Suicidal Customer Procedure

For VAIB COps & TMs

Stage 1: üí≠ Thoughts

Customer has expressed thoughts of harm. Your task is to assess by asking questions about their thoughts and try to confirm if there is intent to harm. Remember, thoughts of harm don't always include specific intent, so asking the right questions is key.

Ask questions like: "Can you tell me more about how you're feeling?" or "I'm sorry you're feeling this way. Can you help me understand more?"

Pause the chat for up to 30 minutes, using three 10-minute intervals. After each 10-minute pause, check for a response before pausing again.

Ask directly if the customer feels safe. If they don't confirm safety or remain unresponsive, escalate the situation.

Stage 2: üìÉ Intent

Customer has expressed intent to harm themselves or others. Ask more focused questions to confirm their intent. Based on the response, you may need to escalate the situation further.

Questions like: "Do you have a plan?" or "Are you thinking of doing something now?" can help confirm the seriousness of the situation.

Pause the chat for up to 30 minutes to allow the customer time to respond. Use three intervals of 10 minutes each, checking in between.

If intent is confirmed, assess the risk and confirm the customer's safety. If no response or confirmation, consider escalating.

Stage 3: üíõ Means

The customer has disclosed they have the means to harm themselves. Investigate if they have a timeframe and prepare to take appropriate actions if necessary.

Ask if they have made any preparations, such as "Have you bought anything to harm yourself?" or "Have you written a note?"

Pause for 10 minutes to give the customer time to respond. Repeat this up to 30 minutes before taking further action.

If means are confirmed, or the customer becomes unresponsive, you may need to escalate and contact emergency services.

Stage 4: üïù Timeframe

Customer has provided a specific timeframe for their planned action. This is a critical stage, and you should confirm safety and prepare to escalate to emergency services if needed.

If the customer provides a timeframe, confirm they feel safe until that time. If not, escalate to emergency services immediately.

If the customer is at imminent risk, call emergency services (999) and provide all relevant details to ensure their safety.
